Our Story

Since 2000, Belitung has lost tens of thousands of hectares of irreplaceable primary forest, with peaks of clearance driven by permanent agriculture and commodity expansion. These losses have left only fragile fragments of natural forest, the last strongholds of biodiversity. Yet within these remnants, the silvered langur (Trachypithecus cristatus) continues to leap among the trees, living side by side with local communities who depend on the same landscapes.

Founded in 2026, the Lutong Conservation Project was born from this reality: a recognition that protecting the langur means protecting life itself. Rooted in science and community collaboration, our journey is both evidence‑based and deeply human, working to safeguard forests, empower local people, and ensure that wildlife and communities can thrive into the future.

Our Vision and Mission

Vision: To become a science‑based wildlife conservation NGO with the silvered langur (Trachypithecus cristatus) as an umbrella species in the Belitung Island ecosystem, through sustainable approaches rooted in collaboration and the empowerment of local communities.

Mission:

1. Develop scientific research on wildlife ecology and biosciences, with a focus on the silvered langur (Trachypithecus cristatus) as an umbrella species in the Belitung Island ecosystem.

2. Implement science communication through environmental education, public campaigns, volunteer programs, and scientific publications to strengthen public awareness of wildlife conservation in Belitung.

3. Create community‑based conservation models by empowering local communities in Belitung as key actors, through sustainable alternative economic development and capacity building.

4. Promote stronger regulations and policies via evidence‑based advocacy that bridges communities, academics, and government in wildlife conservation efforts across Belitung Island.
